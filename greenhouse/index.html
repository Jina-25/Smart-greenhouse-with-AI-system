Smart Multi-crop Greenhouse with AI monitoring system

About the Project

The Smart Multi-Crop Greenhouse with AI monitoring system is an intelligent agricultural system designed to optimize plant growth through real-time environmental monitoring and automated control. The project focuses on managing key factors such as temperature, humidity, and airflow using IoT sensors and smart ventilation mechanisms. A core feature of the system is its ability to switch between different crop profiles, including Stevia, Basil, and Spinach. Each plant has its own predefined environmental requirements stored in a dedicated database. Once a crop is selected, the system automatically adjusts the greenhouse conditions to match its optimal growth parameters. This approach improves efficiency, reduces human intervention, and enhances crop quality and yield. By combining IoT technology with adaptive control systems, the project demonstrates a scalable solution for modern agriculture, supporting sustainable farming practices and smarter resource management.

Stevia

Stevia is a sweet sugar substitute that is about 50 to 300 times sweeter than sugar. It is extracted from the leaves of Stevia rebaudiana, a plant native to areas of Paraguay and Brazil. The active compounds in stevia are steviol glycosides. Stevia is heat-stable, pH-stable, and not fermentable.

Basil

Basil (Ocimum basilicum) is a versatile, aromatic herb in the mint family (Lamiaceae) . Known as the "king of herbs," it is a staple in Italian and Southeast Asian cuisines. Basil is a popular herb used in cooking. It requires good sunlight and moderate watering.

Spinach

Spinach is a leafy green flowering plant native to Central and Western Asia. It is of the order Caryophyllales, family Amaranthaceae, subfamily Chenopodioideae. Its leaves are a common vegetable consumed either fresh, cooked or after storage.
